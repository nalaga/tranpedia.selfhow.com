Hakuraku Station (I get exfoliation and come) is a Tokyu Corporation Toyoko Line station in Hakuraku, Kanagawa-ku, Yokohama-shi, Kanagawa. The station number is TY18.

Station structure

In 2002, the west exit (cf. image) left to the Rokukakubashi area in the home of the Shibuya area was established. The timetable was installed in the late 2000s, but the installed train approach indicator is always used at the same time. This is displayed at the time of a train approach saying "a train comes", and I am displayed with yellow in the passage train more by the lower part saying "this train does not stop" [the source required].

Origin of the station name

Because the cattle appraiser (ばくろう.a cattle appraiser, the person who writes it with the cattle appraiser, and treats a horse, and acts as broker) of the Kanagawa hotel lived a lot in around station, a cattle appraiser turned and became Hakuraku (exfoliation). The place name in connection with horse trading exists in the various parts of Japan. It is unrelated to Bai Juyi.
