Career

I am born in Tosayamadacho, Kami-gun, Kochi (existing Kami-shi) as the eldest daughter of the dentist. I want to be a psychiatrist and take an entrance examination for the public medical department, but, via private Tosa Senior High School, fail. Because mother objects to an unemployed person, I belong to Kyoto Women's University Japanese literature subject. I take an entrance examination for the medical department again, but fail. The further unemployed person is not forgiven and enters the Japanese girl hygiene junior college special course dental health course [1]. I go to Tokyo and enter the dormitory of the campus. It was the life of four people to 6 tatami. I graduate from a university and pass a national board dental hygiene examination. I stayed in Tokyo while working part-time as a dental hygienist and I did entrance to school [2] in Meiji University department of literature literature department French literature specialty and received instruction of Masanao Saito and learned from Mitsuo Nakamura afterwards. I apply for President of Meiji University Prize during attendance at school in "Communist Party". I took up "Being and Nothigness" of Sartre in the graduation thesis [3]. "The Communist Party" was reprinted in "文學界" and became a candidate of the Akutagawa Prize in the first half in 1960.
