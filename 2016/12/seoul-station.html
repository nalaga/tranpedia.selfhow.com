History

 
Seoul Station old station building (historic spot 284th)

Seoul Station started a business as "Namdaemun (Namdaemun) 京仁鉄道合資会社 (current 京仁線) station" on July 8, 1900. The station name is associated with South Gate in the outskirts.

京仁線 inaugurated on September 18, 1899 was the route that linked Port Inchon to capital, the Han town, but heron beam Tsu Station just before it of the Han became the terminal in opening to traffic those days, and the communication to the Han town was bad. On July 5, 1900, a railroad came to be finally in the Han town by the opening to traffic of the Han iron bridge, but the Namdaemun station at the time was wooden construction barrack of the size of 10 tsubos that there was in a rice field of the just outside the city limits. A dragon mountain station had a bigger scale, and South Gate Station was only the supporting station which played its part in those days. Even if Gyeongui Railway Line was inaugurated in the starting point at dragon mountain Station in Kyoto pot line, 1906, the following day, and the train which went through the Korean Peninsula came to go along dragon mountain Station, I remained in the small station of the middle of the cecum line which yet parted from dragon mountain Station for Namdaemun Station in 1905.

South Gate Station was not a terminal station, and, at the time of the opening of business, in the real starting point, it was in "the Keijo (Reeves' muntjac loss) our station station" where the north (the neighborhood of current pear flower girls' high school) matched. After having been renamed to "west Daimon (ソデムン) station" on March 27, 1905, this station is abolished on March 31, 1919.

In 1910, capital Han castle was renamed to Keijo by Japan-Korea merger. As the population of the Keijo prefecture increased, the need to make the central Keijo station which became the doorway increased. The starting point and dragon mountain Station of the origin of Kyoto pot line, Gyeongui Railway Line, Kyoto line where it was were built by a military use at center Station at the time and were far from the city and were unsuitable for a new station building. Therefore, I will rebuild existing South Gate Station near the center of commerce as new Keijo prefecture center Station. New line construction to do the Gyeongui Railway Line which went along the dragon mountain line in South Gate Station way began, and the construction of the new station building by the design of Yasushi Tsukamoto who learned from Kingo Tatsuno in 1922 began till then. It was renamed to "Keijo (けいじょう) station" on January 1, 1923 [1], and, in 1925, the station building of the left red brick was completed now. It was the station building of the scale of the Orient second next to Tokyo Station of the Kingo Tatsuno design in those days. Keijo Station treats the transnational train of the Manchurian area and will take a central role in the transport by rail of the Korean Peninsula.

4号線も島式ホーム1面2線を有する地下駅で、フルスクリーンタイプのホームドアが設置されている。1号線と乗り換える場合、ホーム北端にある階段を上り、乗り換え通路を通る必要がある[9]。

空港鉄道の駅舎はKORAILの駅舎の南西部に位置し、両駅舎は繋がっている。駅舎は地上2階、地下7階の構造で、ホームは地下7階にある。2015年3月、ソウルメトロ ソウル駅方面への地下通路が開通し、1号線、4号線への乗り換え時間が短縮された[10]。

ホームやコンコースは直通列車と一般列車で分離されており、地下2階に直通列車の改札口と都心空港ターミナルが、地下3階に一般列車の改札口がある。ホームは2面3線構造で、島式1面2線を一般列車が使用し、単式1面1線を直通列車が使用する。エスカレーターが稼動しているが地下深いためエレベーターのほうが便利である。

この地下2階にある都心空港ターミナルは大韓航空、アシアナ航空、チェジュ航空、ティーウェイ航空[11]の利用者に限り、ここで搭乗手続きや出国審査ができる。搭乗手続きと出国審査を経た後に直通列車を利用して仁川国際空港に行くことができる。

なお、仁川国際空港駅発着のKTXは、空港鉄道の駅からではなく、地上のKORAILホームより発着する。
