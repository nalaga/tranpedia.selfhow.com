History

A flute was a wind instrument of the gagaku mainly, and shakuhachi, 簫 (occur) were used other than current dragon flute (dragon flute), sho, flageolet-like instrument, Kokuryo flute, kagura flute in Nara, the Heian era, too. Of these, a sho, the flageolet-like instrument come to be distinguished from the flute later. Because the kagura flute is invited to a Japanese flute, it is thought that there has been already a flute in Japan before a dragon flute and a Kokuryo flute are transmitted from the continent. In fact, the remains which are supposedly a flute are excavated from the Tenri-shi, Nara star mound one old burial mound (last part of Burial Mound age). But this has many points unlike the shape of the flute after the Nara era, too.

A bamboo flute is frequent in a festival (festival music), a noh dance (ordinary kagura), the lion dance of the common people, but there is the district using a dragon flute and the ability pipe, too.
