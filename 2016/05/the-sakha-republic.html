The Russian (race) used the Lena water system as a street to the Far East including the Sea of Okhotsk and collected a fur for tax for Sakha and other races and established a monetary economy for the cause in this. The light industries and the traffic such as steamships began to develop early in the Soviet Union from the end of 19th century, and underground resources such as the money were found more, and the mining industry began, too.

On April 27, 1922, the Soviet Union made "Yakut Soviet Union socialism self-government republic" replacing with previous "ヤコルスカヤ district", but White Army ruled over the eastern part of Yakut substantially including capital Yakutsk during Russia civil war period, and Yakut revolt got up for 1921 through 1923. Under the Joseph Stalin system, people sent to the forced labor place (グラグ) engaged in highway construction, mine labor, deforestion in Yakut, and a majority lost the life. In 1992, Yakut after the Soviet Union collapse was approved as "Sakha (ヤクーチア) republic" in the Russian Federation.
