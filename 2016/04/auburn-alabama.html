History

Residential area (about 1830) of the indigenous people in Alabama

A native American creek group settled down in this whole area with today's Auburn in eastern Alabama before the European settlement. In 1832, this ground was performed cession of from a creek group in the United States of America by a treaty of カセッタ. When the creek group which remained in this ground by the second creek war in 1836 four years later was ousted by an order of President Andrew Jackson at the time, John J Harper of the Methodist founded a town in this ground. This town got the approval of the Alabama state legislature before long in 1839 and was become a corporation formally [1].

Auburn developed from the early days as a center of the education. In 1840, Auburn Women's College (existing Auburn Senior High School) founded it in form to reorganize a school of Methodist who had been already founded line [3]. As private liberal arts Courage affiliated with Methodist communion in 1856, east Alabama Men's College which was a forerunner of the Auburn University founded it [4]. However, when the Civil War began, these universities became the closing a school, too and were converted into a field hospital [1].

Even if the Civil War was over, time suffered before the reopening of the university came to be able to manage to come up with an expense to suffer from by a blow to regional economy of Auburn which war brought. The school building of the Auburn Women's College was leased for business for a while after the end of the war and wandered the owner including the chair factory from place to place. When regional economy looked up before long, the Auburn citizen bought back a school building and established a school district and brought back Auburn Women's College as a high school of the coeducation [3].

Auburn University (about 1890)

Although I reopened immediately after the end of the war, students were not readily gathered, and one east Alabama Men's College suffered from a financial difficulty. When the Methodist Church parted with east Alabama Men's College in 1872, the fellow scholar applied the Morrill Act of the federation and was played as State University of land Grant and was renamed to the Alabama A&M University [1]. For the next 1,892 years, the Alabama A&M university accepted a girl student for the first time and became coeducation [4].

After all it was a university to have supported regional economy growth of Auburn from the late 19th century through the 20th century. When the world financial crisis happened in 1929, the regional economy of Auburn was delayed because of high dependence to the university, but the city improved by the later new dealism. A student and the staff of a school increased rapidly after World War II, and the population of the city greatly increased. When the 1990s began, manufacturing industry developed in Auburn and established the position as another axis in the regional economy [1]. And, in 2008, an industry-university co-operation official is public; of the high-tech industry Alabama state government, Auburn City government and Auburn University was public, and opened the Auburn research park in order to be based [5].
