Kiso (きそ) is the fifth warship of the Kuma model light cruiser of the Great Japanese Empire navy. The name of a warship came from Kisogawa that drifted to the Tokai district and was named.

Summary

Activity

In 1921, I am completed in Nagasaki. I participated in Siberia withdrawal of troops and Japan-China War after the completion, but I will engage in a northern strategy after the Pacific War outbreak of war as the fifth fleet position and participate in Attu Island, キスカ island capture strategy with the Operation Midway in 1942 and キスカ island disengaging action in the next year. I mainly got a transportation duty afterwards.

mounted with a plane

I installed a slide before a hangar and a bridge deck under a bridge deck at the time of the completion and allowed it to accommodate a land plane. Therefore a bridge deck structure upsized than other Kuma models. A bridge deck structure makes a following Nagara type, the Kawauchi type big for a similar reason. However, the utility of the slide was poor, and after that the land plane was not equipped with removal later.

The other 5,500 tons types light cruiser set up a catapult at the time of modernization remodeling, and it was equipped with one seaplane, but, in Kiso, the seaplane was not equipped with without installing a catapult with Ooi, northing.

Rainwater removal device

Kiso installs an abacus-shaped rainwater removal device in the upper one or two turn chimneys and can distinguish it from other warships for this purpose easily. In addition, Kuma sets up an abacus-shaped thing to all three chimneys with other 5,500 tons types, and it is at the identification point with other warships.

Warship career

On June 10, 1919, I start work. 1920December 14、The launching。 On May 4, 1921, I was completed in Mitsubishi shipbuilding Nagasaki shipyard (existing Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Nagasaki shipyard).

I participated in the Siberia dispatch of troops and I was based in Port Arthur successively and acted as the patrol of the coast between Kanto State and Qingdao.

On February 26, 1936, he/she cancels the dispatch by a request of the Naval General Staff on the occasion of a 2.26 case though I am going to achieve urgent dispatch to Tokyo Bay by the judgment of the Yokosuka naval station by the end of the day.

I fired the salute of 21 verges that Yoneshige cruiser Astoria carried ashes of Japanese ambassador Hiroshi Saito to the United States on board on April 17, 1939, and arrived at Yokosuka.

Pacific War

Kiso was incorporated into the 21st squadron of the fifth fleet (1941) on November 10 in 1941. I was active at the time of the Pearl Harbor attack in the Chishima Islands and came back to Yokosuka for the damage caused by the stormy weather at the end of the same year. Kiso was active (1942) at northern area of the sea with Tama of the warship of the same type from January to April in 1942.

When the dough liter air raid by U.S. forces was carried out in April, Kiso was dispatched for the task force chase in the United States, too. I discovered the monitoring boat "the 21st southing circle" that Kiso received bombing on 19th and was damaged and was drifting and disposed by bombardment after the help of the crew [2].
