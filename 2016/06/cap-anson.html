The player that cap Anson (Adrian Constantine "Cap" Anson, from April 17, 1852 to April 14, 1922) played an active part in United States Major League in the 19th century. The main position is a first baseman.

"The cap" comes from that I was called "a captain" from the strong leadership by a nickname.

Table of contents

Brief career history

In 1871, I make my debut in National association (NA) which was a league having just finished starting in those days. I played in Rockford Forrest City's and Philadelphia Athletics (unrelated to current Oakland Athletics). Director player additional post served in 1875 of Athletics Era, too.

In 1876, I transfer to Chicago white stockings by drawing against the agreement between leagues in National League start to continue now as a league of MLB. I play an active part as director player additional post of white stockings from 1879. I achieve five times of league conquest to 1886.

For a supervisor, I minded environment so that other players could always make every possible effort. The excellent player such as King Kelly and George Gore plays with Anson, too. In addition, I am known as the person who established the pitching rotation by maintenance of the tactics such as hit and runs and plural pitchers, team management art performed commonly now including the introduction of the spring training.

It was the best as a batter in such a strong team and was the player who recorded 24 steals at the age of 44 years old, and was long-standing. The active play lasts 27 years after making the debut at 19 years old and records 3,000 hits becoming first among MLB in history. In addition, I record eight times of league most RBIs becoming the MLB most. I retire in 1897. I retired from the supervision assumption of office of Chicago at the same time.

I refused the game with the team except the white for celebrity as a racist firmly [1]. I am said to be the person who established "a color line" of MLB. In 1922, I die of an illness in Chicago.

In 1939, I am elected in the American baseball palace by Veterans Committee. It became with the first palace with back Ewing for a player of the 19th century.
