The traffic science museum (こうつうかがくはくぶつかん, English: Modern Transportation Museum) is traffic, a museum about the science which was ever at 3-11-10, Namiyoke, Minato-ku, Osaka-shi, Osaka.

It was in the underpass of Osaka Loop Line Bentencho Station, and West Japan Railway Co. (West Japan Railway) owned it, and a public interest traffic culture promotion foundation Foundation ran it.

History

Museum of Transportation where the traffic-related museum in Japan was in Kandasudacho of Tokyo in the early 30s of the Showa era was the only thing. The Japanese National Railways which owned a museum considered whether I install an annex of Museum of Transportation in Osaka District in about 1957, but will install it afterwards as a museum mainly on the display about the traffic in the future as an annex of Museum of Transportation in the present age when is different from Museum of Transportation in the concept.

The opening is realized as Osaka Loop Line completion commemorative project and next will be established Bentencho Station as "the traffic Hall of Science"; October 14, 1961 (preparations were pushed forward for the opening of 〉) on the day of 〈 existing railroad in an anniversary of railway transportation. However, construction was late by the second Muroto typhoon of September of the year, and the opening was opened on postponement, January 21, 1962, the following day. It was a name of "the Hall of Science" at first, but receives the designation of "museum considerable facilities" to fix for museum method Article 29 in March, 1964 [1].

In the beginning of opening, the historic display about the railroad was held in check by process such as the above, and the preservation display of the real railroad carriage was only one steam locomotive and three passenger cars, too. Some stores product about the field of traffic except the railroad moves to this museum afterwards because it was the display constitution that succession and the railroad Museum that it was (Saitama-shi) specialized in the field of railroad according to the name after the historic showpiece is added other than a railroad carriage sequentially, and Museum of Transportation closed at 2006.

The administration at the beginning of opening was consigned to Japan Travel Bureau Foundation by Japanese National Railways, but was changed to the traffic culture promotion foundation which started as an affiliated organization of Japanese National Railways in 1970 [2]. After Japanese National Railways division and privatization, West Japan Railway and East Japan Railway Co. (JR East) succeeded the foundation jointly, but they moved the headquarters in this museum on August 1, 2009 after the Museum of Transportation closing and made a fresh start as the associated organization which independent, West Japan Railway took charge of.

However, the deterioration of facilities, facilities have become small, and build the new railroad Museum in form to expand the plum alley steam locomotive building in Shimogyo-ku, Kyoto-shi [3], and, by 2016, will close a traffic science museum on (in December, 2013 in "Kyoto railroad Museum" name decision [4]), April 6, 2014. As for the store document of the hall, the development of facilities becomes going to be stimulated as part of "Osaka Loop Line remodeling project" about the future usage after the closing [5] to transfer by a new museum. It was 2015, and, about a part of the display vehicle (D51 form steam locomotive, DD13 form diesel, DF50 form diesel), it was announced not Kyoto railroad Museum what was transferred by Tsuyama まなびの railroad building of Tsuyama-shi, Okayama (Tsuyama sector engine garage) [6].

The number of the people of entering a building of 2008 was 289,800 people.

A bureau of this museum was set up in South Gate Building after Osaka station city opened in May, 2011. From July 19, 2014 after the closing to August 17, was shown display a part of the old stored possession in promotion "Fun Fan Festa 2014" in the summer of the facilities [7]; [8].

The exhibit space was three of indoor exhibit space, outdoor exhibit space, the second exhibit space. I explain display constitution at the time of the closing as follows.

There was the popular "model railroad panorama room" in the depths in this museum. A one-80th scale 16th gauge railroad model vehicles such as Shinkansen train or the sleeper train ran in a huge diorama. A curator operated a centralized control board and drove by manual operation while adding sleeve notes [11].
