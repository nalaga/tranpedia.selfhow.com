The generic name of a series of figure skating tournaments in the youth class where the 2013/2014 ISU Junior Grand Prix series (English: 2013/2014 ISU Junior Grand Prix of Figure Skating) was held in eight countries of the world in 2013.

Table of contents

Summary

The ISU Junior Grand Prix of the 2013-2014 season. Unlike the ISU Grand Prix of Figure Skating of the senior class, the site changes every year. I am comprised of Latvia, Mexico, Slovakia, Poland, Belarus, Czech, 7 meetings of Estonia and the final, the ISU Junior Grand Prix finals held in Japan this season.

The player whom a figure skating unification group chose of each country participates and performs a man and woman single, a pair, an ice dancing competition. But it is only 5 meetings during 7 meetings that a pair is performed. The holding of the pair became 5 meetings for the first time in our season.

The pair is performed in 5 meetings except the Mexican cup and バルティック cup among 7 meetings. In addition, the finals are held with the grand prix finals of the senior at the same time.

Participation qualification

A participation frame is given for an area each country, and a local unification group decides the starter each country. I can participate in each meeting except the finals until 2 meetings for one player (group).

In addition, the player (group) participating in the grand prix series cannot participate in this Junior Grand Prix. An additional post was recognized about the pair until the last year, but cannot participate in the grand prix series of the senior including a pair in this year.
