Logan county (Logan County, a standard abbreviation: LG) is a county located in United States of America Kansas. It was 2,756 people, and the population in the 2010 national census decreased 9.5% from 3,046 people of 2000 [1]. The most big city of this county and the county seat are Oakley [2]. The county name was named for John A General Logan [3].

Law and government

Kansas constitution was revised in 1986, but the Logan county forbids alcohol until 2006;, in other words, remained a county of "dry". This year, inhabitants approved the sale of the alcoholic drinks which an individual enjoyed by a vote. There was not the limit for the food volume [4].
