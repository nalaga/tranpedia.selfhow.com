Okinawa stamp (aviation stamp of the 1951 publication)

The stamp that the Okinawa stamp (I get up and cut a rope) was used in Okinawa under the U.S. forces rule. I call it the Ryukyu stamp (りゅうきゅうきって), but may use Okinawa stamp and the Ryukyu stamp in a different meaning to mention it later. In Japan Post, I use the word the Ryukyu stamp, but this is a meaning of the wide sense. Except some exceptions, the Japanese Ministry of Finance Printing Bureau (current National Printing Bureau) printed the Okinawa stamp. It is a dead country.

Okinawa stamp (Ryukyu stamp) of the wide sense

I point to the stamp published by mainland return of May 14, 1972 in Okinawa from the Okinawa occupation of U.S. forces of 1945. A letter called "Ryukyu mail" becomes available.

Okinawa stamp of the narrow sense

Summary

I added Amami groups of islands that were Kagoshima position, and Okinawa after the Okinawa war end was governed separately in four districts. The thing which I stamped with a private seal and a seal of approval of the civil administration prefecture Director of Communications Division of each group of islands was used for the stock of the prewar stamp then. What I stamped with a seal is to distinguish it from a stamp of the stock which a commoner had. I order a new stamp from Japan expressly for last years, and there is the thing which stamped this with a seal. Therefore, there are a great many kinds, but there are few existing things.

In Kumejima, there is a stamp (Kumejima stamp) which a postmaster gets the permission of the United States Armed Forces, and made prints with mimeographs originally.

From July 1, 1948, an original design and a stamp by the "Ryukyu mail" indication came to be published. When four group of islands government was gathered up in 1951, and Ryukyu provisional government was made, a Ryukyu postal administration agency to gather up mail business was made, and Ryukyu provisional government suffered from the mail the jurisdiction of the Ryukyu government when the Ryukyu government was established on April 1, 1952, the following day, and the Ryukyu stamp with the letter of "the Ryukyu mail" was published afterwards.

Because the Ryukyu stamp was the design which reflected Okinawa original culture, popularity increased as a year passed and played a big role in foreign currency acquisition. However, use was stopped with return of Okinawa on June 3, 1972.

Some suppliers of the mainland fanned it from 1971 through 1973 saying "it is not published in future by return" and they could stand in line for a stamp published in a speculation purpose newly very much and became a social phenomenon. Buying suppliers from the mainland clustered at last in front of a post office, and there was the case which I bought at a price of 3 times of the net price.
