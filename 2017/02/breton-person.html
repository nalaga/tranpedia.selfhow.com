The Breton person (French: Bretons, Breton: Breizhiz or Bretoned) is a race of Celts who mainly live in France, the Bretagne district line. Their ancestors are the yellowtail ton people that they emigrated to from the southwestern part of Great Britain over the fourth to the sixth century. The place name Bretagne is associated with them, and some people still speak pro-Celtic Breton (recently called the Blaise word). The community of the Breton person is in French overseas Canadian (mainly Quebec) United States of America, U.K., Ireland.
