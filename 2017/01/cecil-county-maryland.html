Cecil county (British: Cecil County) is a county located in the northeast corner of United States of America Maryland. It was 101,108 people, and the population in the 2010 national census rose 17.6% from 85,951 people of 2000 [1]. County seat is a city of the population maximum in the county that is an elk ton city (a population of 15,443 people [2]). The county name was adopted from Baron the second Baltimore セシリウス Calvert that was Governor Maryland colony first generation feudal lord from 1632 to 1675 of the death.
