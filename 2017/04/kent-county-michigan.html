Kent county (British: Kent County) is a county located in the western part of the United States of America Michigan ロウアー peninsula. It was 602,622 people, and the population in the 2010 national census rose 4.9% from 574,335 people of 2000 [1]. County seat is a city of the population maximum in the county that is Grand Rapids city (a population of 188,040 people [2]). The county name was named Ohio for jurist James Kent of New York that acted as the representative of the Michigan associate state at the time of the Toledo war that disputed it.

The Kent county belongs to the Grand Rapids Wyoming metropolis. There is steel case company in the county and is economy of west Michigan and the industrial center. In addition, there is the Frederick May L garden which is a cultural symbol of the middle western district. It was the republican strong ground and was in the conservative soil traditionally, but supported Presidential Candidate Democratic Party in the United States of America presidential election in 2008. This became first in 44 years since 1964. Even as to religion is conservative, and there are a significant number of evangelism Christians. There is Gerald R Ford International Airport in a county.
