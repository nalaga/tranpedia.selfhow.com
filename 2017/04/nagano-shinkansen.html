Nagano Shinkansen (the Nagano Shinkansen) is the popular name which has been ever used for the section which East Japan Railway Co. (JR East) operates in the Hokuriku Shinkansen (driving systematic name).

Takasaki Station (in the operating system top Tokyo Station) - Nagano Station interval of the Hokuriku Shinkansen was inaugurated to the holding of the Nagano Olympics of February (1997) (1998) on October 1 in 1997 in 1998, but was guided "Nagano line Shinkansen" (the Nagano breath Shinkansen) with "Nagano Shinkansen" later conveniently because I was not connected by this point in time to the Hokuriku district. When I practiced medicine (2015) to Kanazawa Station on March 14 in 2015, the names in the guidance were unified to "the Hokuriku Shinkansen" of the official name, and the name called Nagano Shinkansen became extinct. After that notation called "the Hokuriku Shinkansen" (via Nagano) may be used to state that I go by way of Nagano clearly in the JR East section (the details of the change of the name mention it later).
